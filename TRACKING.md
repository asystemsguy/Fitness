**How to Measure:**
- **Waist:** Tape at belly button, exhale normally, don't suck in
- **Chest:** Tape across nipples, arms at sides
- **Hips:** Tape at widest part of hips/glutes

**Why Measurements Matter:**
- Scale weight can fluctuate daily (water, food, hormones)
- Measurements show fat loss even when weight stalls
- Muscle weighs more than fat (volume vs weight)

## Apple Watch Setup Guide

### Recommended Apps & Settings

**1. Activity App (Built-in)**
- Set Move goal: 500-700 active calories
- Set Exercise goal: 30 minutes
- Set Stand goal: 12 hours
- Enable notifications for stand reminders

**2. Workout App (Built-in)**
- Create custom workouts:
  - "Gym Strength" (45 min)
  - "Home Dumbbells" (30 min)
  - "Cardio" (30 min)
  - "Core" (15 min)
- Enable heart rate monitoring
- Auto-pause when you stop

**3. Heart Rate App (Built-in)**
- Check resting heart rate weekly (morning, before getting out of bed)
- Trend should decrease over time (better cardiovascular fitness)

**4. Sleep App (Built-in)**
- Set sleep schedule: 10:30 PM - 6:30 AM (or your preference)
- Enable sleep tracking
- Review sleep stages weekly

**5. Third-Party Apps (Optional)**
- **MyFitnessPal:** Track calories and macros (syncs with Apple Health)
- **Water Minder:** Track water intake
- **Strong:** Log gym workouts with weights/reps
- **Zones:** Heart rate zone training

### Apple Health Integration

**Enable These Data Sources:**
- Steps (from Apple Watch)
- Workouts (from Workout app)
- Weight (manual entry or compatible scale)
- Nutrition (from MyFitnessPal or manual)
- Sleep (from Sleep app)
- Heart Rate (from Apple Watch)

**How to Check:**
iPhone → Health app → Browse → Select category → Add Data

## Weight Tracking Best Practices

### Understanding Weight Fluctuations

**Daily fluctuations are NORMAL (±1-2 kg):**
- Water retention (sodium, carbs, hormones)
- Food in digestive system
- Muscle glycogen storage
- Inflammation from workouts
- Bowel movements

**When to Weigh:**
✅ Morning, after bathroom, before eating/drinking  
✅ Same scale, same location  
✅ Minimal clothing  
✅ Record immediately  

**When NOT to Weigh:**
❌ After meals  
❌ Before bed  
❌ After intense workout (dehydration)  
❌ Different scales (gym vs home)  

### How to Interpret Weight Data

**Look at WEEKLY AVERAGE, not daily numbers:**

| Day | Weight | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| Mon | 83.2 kg | |
| Tue | 82.8 kg | -0.4 kg |
| Wed | 83.5 kg | +0.7 kg (water retention) |
| Thu | 83.0 kg | -0.5 kg |
| Fri | 82.6 kg | -0.4 kg |
| Sat | 82.9 kg | +0.3 kg |
| Sun | 82.7 kg | -0.2 kg |
| **Weekly Avg** | **82.96 kg** | **-0.24 kg from last week** ✓ |

**Good Progress:** 0.5-1.0 kg loss per week (average)

**If Weight Loss Stalls (2+ weeks):**
1. Check if measurements are still decreasing (might be body recomposition)
2. Review food tracking (creeping calories?)
3. Increase cardio by 10 min/session
4. Reduce carbs slightly (100-150g less per day)
5. Check sleep quality (poor sleep = weight loss resistance)

**If Losing Too Fast (>1.5 kg/week):**
- Increase calories by 200-300 (add carbs/fats)
- Ensure protein is 140g+ (preserve muscle)
- You're losing muscle, not just fat

## Motivation & Accountability

### Why Track Everything?

1. **Awareness:** You can't improve what you don't measure
2. **Patterns:** See what works (e.g., better sleep = better workouts)
3. **Accountability:** Hard to skip workout when you're tracking streaks
4. **Motivation:** Seeing progress (even small) keeps you going
5. **Adjustments:** Data tells you when to change course

### When You Miss a Day

**Don't:**
- Beat yourself up
- Skip tracking entirely
- Think "I failed, might as well quit"

**Do:**
- Log it as a rest day or missed day (honesty matters)
- Get back on track the NEXT day
- Remember: One missed day ≠ failure
- Ask: "What caused this? How can I prevent it?"

### Celebrate Milestones

**Non-Scale Victories to Celebrate:**
